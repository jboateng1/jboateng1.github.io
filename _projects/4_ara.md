> **ARA** is the NSF PAWR program's rural-focused wireless living lab — a multi-site, programmable infrastructure spanning **central Iowa** that gives researchers a real-world testbed for 5G/6G, Open RAN, edge computing, and rural broadband.

#### <i class="fa-solid fa-bullseye"></i>&nbsp; Vision

Wireless research has a _reality gap_: most innovations are validated in simulation or single-room testbeds, then fail to translate to deployment. ARA closes that gap by giving researchers a programmable, instrumented infrastructure that lives outdoors — across farms, towns, and the long fiber/microwave links that connect them.

#### <i class="fa-solid fa-screwdriver-wrench"></i>&nbsp; My role

I lead the **end-to-end 5G and Open RAN platform** within ARA — turning a heterogeneous SDR/x-Haul/edge testbed into an experiment-ready environment for external researchers. This includes the AraSDR platform, AraRACH reliability work, and the ARA-O-RAN extensions for agriculture and rural connectivity.
